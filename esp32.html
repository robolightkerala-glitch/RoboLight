At ROBOLIGHT, we harness the low-power dual-core performance of the ESP32 chip. Engineered for real-time robotic motor driving, wireless telemetry, and IoT controls.

ESP32 Core Specifications

Dual-Core Processor

Tensilica Xtensa 32-bit LX6 dual-core microprocessors running at up to 240 MHz for high-speed sensor fusion and real-time processing.

Wireless Connectivity

Integrated 2.4 GHz Wi-Fi (802.11 b/g/n) and Bluetooth v4.2 BR/EDR & BLE for seamless remote drone and robot manipulation.

Power Efficiency

Ultra-low power (ULP) co-processor allows continuous sensor monitoring while keeping overall battery power consumption minimal.

Memory & Storage

520 KB SRAM with external flash memory support up to 16 MB to store custom firmware, WebSockets, and control scripts.

ESP32 Pinout & Sensor Interfaces

Flexible Peripherals

Designed for compact robotics modules. Wire up IMUs, motor bridges, ultrasonic sensors, and wireless transceivers directly.

  • 36 GPIO Pins: Capacitive touch sensing, ADC (Analog-to-Digital), and DAC channels.
  • PWM Control (LEDC): Precise Multi-channel PWM signals to control servos, ESCs, and DC motors.
  • Bus Protocols: Multiple SPI, I2C, and UART channels for multi-sensor setups.
  • Built-in Sensors: Integrated Hall effect sensor and temperature measurement capabilities.
